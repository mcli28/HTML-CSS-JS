The apple forms a tree that is small and deciduos, reaching 3 to 13 metres (9.8 to 39 ft) tall, with a broad, often densely twiggy crown.
The leaves are alternately arranged simple ovals 5 to 12 cm long and 3-6 centimeters (1.2-2.4 in) broad on a 2 to 5 centimetres (0.79 to 2.0 in) petiole with anacute tip, serrated margin and a slighty downy undeside. Blossoms are produced in spring simultaneously with the budding of the leaves.
The flowers are white with a pink tinge that gradually fades, five petaled, and 2.5 to 3.5 centimetres (0.98 to 1.4 in) in diameter. The fruit matures in autumn, and is typically 5 to 9 centimetres ( 2.0 to 3.5 in) in diameter.
The center of the fruit contains five carpels arranged in a five-point star, each carpel containing one to three seeds, called pips.
